Ten ways to show the projects without the disturbing left-to-right drift. Green = calm (safe for motion-sensitive eyes & old devices) · amber = a little motion. Scroll, hover, click, swipe — test each, then tell me your favorites.

01Reveal GridCalmest

Cards rest in a tidy grid and fade up as you scroll to them — then stay perfectly still. Zero drift, very premium, flawless on every device. My top pick for "classy + safe."

02Hover-Lift GridCalm

Same still grid, but each card lifts and glows only when you point at it. Motion exists — but it answers to you, never runs on its own. Feels alive and expensive.

03Cross-Fade RotatorSoft motion

Shows three projects, then dissolves to the next three every few seconds. Things appear and fade in place — nothing ever slides across your eye, so it doesn't trigger that seasick feeling.

04Fade Carousel + DotsSoft motion

One featured project at a time, fading from one to the next, with dots you can tap to jump. Elegant and minimal — and the dots hand control back to the viewer.

05Arrow CarouselCalm

Cards sit still; you press ‹ › to glide through them (smooth scroll-snap). No automatic motion at all — it only moves when asked. Great control, zero nausea.

06Swipe StripCalm · mobile-first

Drag / swipe to browse, with magnetic snap. Feels native on a phone and totally still on desktop until you grab it. Best when most visitors are on mobile.

07Accordion ListCalmest

A clean vertical list; click a row to expand its detail. Compact, content-rich, library-quiet. Excellent on small screens and old hardware — almost no rendering cost.

08Flip CardsClick motion

Tidy grid; click a card and it flips to reveal the description on its back. Playful, tactile, and the motion only happens on your click — never ambient.

09Spotlight + ThumbnailsCalm

One big featured panel beside a list of thumbnails — click a thumb to load it into the spotlight (gentle fade). Editorial, confident, magazine-like. Puts a flagship project front and center.

10ConstellationCalm

The old orbit's calm cousin: projects placed as a static star-cluster with faint connecting lines; nodes brighten on hover. The "ecosystem" feeling — without anything spinning. (Stacks to a grid on phones.)
